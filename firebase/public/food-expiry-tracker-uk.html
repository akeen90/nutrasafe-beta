The UK Food Waste Problem

UK households throw away approximately £14 billion worth of edible food each year — much of it because people aren't sure if it's still safe to eat.

Forgotten leftovers, opened packets, and confusing date labels contribute to massive food waste. The average UK household wastes around £700 worth of food annually — food that could have been eaten if properly tracked.

NutraSafe's food expiry tracker helps you reduce waste, save money, and stay safe by keeping track of what's in your fridge and cupboard.

Understanding UK Food Date Labels

UK food labelling uses different date indicators:

  • Use by: Important for food safety. Don't eat food after this date, even if it looks and smells fine
  • Best before: About quality, not safety. Food is usually safe after this date but may not be at its best
  • Display until / Sell by: For shop staff, not consumers

The confusion between these labels contributes to unnecessary waste. Many people throw away food that's still perfectly safe to eat.

Tips for Reducing Food Waste

First In, First Out (FIFO)

Use NutraSafe to practise FIFO — always use older items first. The app shows items sorted by expiry date, making it easy to see what needs using.

Freeze Before It's Too Late

When NutraSafe alerts you about approaching expiry dates, consider freezing food instead of letting it spoil. Most foods freeze well before their use-by date.

Plan Meals Around Expiring Items

Check your NutraSafe tracker when planning meals. Build your menu around ingredients that need using soon.

Understand Storage Guidelines

Proper storage extends food life. Keep your fridge at 5°C or below, store items in appropriate containers, and follow "once opened" guidelines on packaging.

Common Foods to Track

Opened Dairy Products

Milk, cream, yoghurt, and cheese all have limited shelf life once opened. Track opening dates and use-by dates to avoid waste.

Leftovers

Cooked food should typically be consumed within 2-3 days. NutraSafe helps you track leftovers so nothing gets forgotten at the back of the fridge.

Fresh Produce

Fruit and vegetables often don't have use-by dates. Set custom dates based on when you bought them and typical shelf life.

Opened Packets

Opened packets of ham, bacon, smoked fish, and other deli items deteriorate quickly. Track opening dates to ensure food safety.
