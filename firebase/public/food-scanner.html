Food Scanner FAQs

What is a food scanner?
A food scanner is a mobile tool that reads barcodes on food packaging to instantly display nutritional information, ingredients, additives, allergens, and health ratings. Simply point your phone camera at any barcode and get detailed information about what's in your food.
How does a food scanner work?
Food scanners use your phone's camera to read the barcode on food packaging. The barcode number is matched against a database of products to retrieve nutritional data, ingredients, additives (E-numbers), allergen warnings, and health scores. Results appear instantly on your screen.
Which UK supermarket products can I scan?
NutraSafe's food scanner works with products from all major UK supermarkets including Tesco, Sainsbury's, Asda, Morrisons, Aldi, Lidl, Waitrose, Co-op, M&S, and more. Our database covers hundreds of thousands of UK products.
Is the food scanner free to use?
Yes, NutraSafe's food scanner is free to download. You can scan barcodes and view nutritional information at no cost. Free users can log up to 5 foods per day. Premium unlocks unlimited logging, detailed additive analysis, and pattern tracking.
Can the food scanner detect allergens?
Yes. The scanner identifies all 14 major UK allergens including gluten, dairy, eggs, nuts, peanuts, soy, fish, shellfish, celery, mustard, sesame, sulphites, lupin, and molluscs. You can set up a personal allergen profile for automatic warnings.
Does it explain E-numbers?
Yes. When you scan a product, NutraSafe identifies all E-numbers and provides plain-language explanations of what they are, their purpose, and any potential health concerns based on scientific research.
