Vitamin Tracking FAQs

Does MyFitnessPal track vitamins?

MyFitnessPal has limited vitamin tracking. The free version only shows calories, protein, carbs, fat, sugar, and fibre. Premium adds some micronutrients but the data is often incomplete because it relies on user-submitted entries. For comprehensive vitamin and mineral tracking, NutraSafe or Cronometer are better choices.

What vitamins should I track daily?

The most important vitamins to track are: Vitamin D (especially in UK winters), Vitamin B12 (critical for vegans/vegetarians), Iron (important for women), Calcium (bone health), Vitamin C (immunity), and Omega-3 (brain health). NutraSafe tracks all of these plus many more.

Is there a free vitamin tracker app?

Yes, NutraSafe is a free vitamin tracker app for iOS. The free version lets you scan unlimited barcodes and log up to 5 foods per day with complete vitamin and mineral tracking. Premium unlocks unlimited logging from just 2.99/month.

What is the difference between Cronometer and NutraSafe?

Both track vitamins and minerals comprehensively. NutraSafe is UK-focused with UK supermarket products, while Cronometer uses mainly US/Canadian databases. NutraSafe also includes food additive scanning, E-number identification, food reaction tracking, expiry date reminders, and fasting timers - features Cronometer doesn't have.

How accurate are vitamin tracker apps?

Accuracy depends on database quality. Apps using verified nutritional databases (NutraSafe, Cronometer) are more accurate than those relying on user-submitted data (MyFitnessPal). For best accuracy: scan barcodes rather than searching generic foods, and check that serving sizes match what you actually ate.

Can I track supplements in a vitamin tracker app?

Yes. In NutraSafe, you can scan supplement barcodes or search for common vitamins and minerals. The app adds the supplement's vitamin content to your daily totals, so you can see your complete intake from both food and supplements.
