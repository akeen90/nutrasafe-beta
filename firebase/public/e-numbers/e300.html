What is E300 (Ascorbic Acid)?

E300, known as Ascorbic Acid or simply Vitamin C, is a water-soluble vitamin and powerful antioxidant used to preserve food and prevent browning.

Why it's used in food:

  • Prevents oxidation ‚Äì stops fruits and vegetables from turning brown
  • Antioxidant ‚Äì protects nutrients, colours, and flavours from degradation
  • Preserves freshness ‚Äì extends shelf life naturally
  • Enhances iron absorption ‚Äì improves bioavailability of iron in foods
  • Fortification ‚Äì adds nutritional value to products
  • Blocks nitrosamine formation ‚Äì prevents carcinogenic compounds forming in cured meats

E300 is essential for human health. Unlike most animals, humans cannot produce vitamin C and must get it from diet. The UK recommended daily intake is 40mg for adults (though many health experts recommend 100-200mg).

  • Immune function ‚Äì supports white blood cells and antibody production
  • Wound healing ‚Äì necessary for tissue repair
  • Antioxidant protection ‚Äì neutralizes free radicals that damage cells

E300 protects cells from oxidative damage:

Potential Side Effects (Only at Very High Doses)

E300 is safe at typical food additive levels, but very high supplemental doses (>2,000mg/day) can cause:

  • Digestive upset ‚Äì diarrhoea, nausea, stomach cramps (usually >2,000mg)
  • Kidney stones ‚Äì rare, only in people with history of kidney stones taking mega-doses (>3,000mg daily)

Context: A typical food product contains 10-100mg of added E300. You'd need to consume 20-200 products to reach concerning levels. This doesn't happen in normal consumption.

UK Recommended Intake:

  • Adults: 40mg per day (minimum to prevent scurvy)
  • Optimal intake: 100-200mg per day (according to many nutritionists)
  • Upper safe limit: 1,000mg per day (UK) / 2,000mg (EU/US)

Natural vs. Synthetic E300

Many people wonder if synthetic E300 is inferior to natural vitamin C. The answer: they're identical.

Chemical Structure

How Synthetic E300 is Made

Most commercial vitamin C is made using the Reichstein process:

  • Starts with glucose (from corn or wheat)
  • Fermented with bacteria to convert to ascorbic acid
  • Purified to pharmaceutical-grade vitamin C
  • Used in supplements and food additives

Are Natural Sources Better?

Some argue natural sources contain additional beneficial compounds (bioflavonoids, phytonutrients). While true, the vitamin C itself is identical. Benefits of natural sources:

  • Contain fibre, bioflavonoids, and other nutrients
  • More balanced nutrition overall

Bottom line: Eat plenty of fruits and vegetables for overall health, but don't fear E300 in foods ‚Äì it's the exact same compound.

Why E300 is Added to Bacon and Ham

You might wonder why vitamin C is added to processed meats like bacon, ham, and sausages. The answer: cancer prevention.

The problem:

  • Processed meats contain sodium nitrite (E250) to prevent botulism
  • Nitrites can form nitrosamines (carcinogenic compounds) during cooking
  • Nitrosamines are linked to bowel and stomach cancer

The solution:

  • Adding vitamin C (E300) or sodium ascorbate (E301) to cured meats
  • Vitamin C blocks nitrosamine formation by up to 90%
  • UK/EU regulations now encourage or require ascorbic acid in nitrite-cured products

Result: E300 makes processed meats safer. If you eat bacon or ham, look for products listing E300/E301 ‚Äì it's protecting you.
