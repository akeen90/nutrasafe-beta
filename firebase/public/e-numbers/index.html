E-Numbers Database UK

Complete guide to all food additives approved in the UK. Search any E-number to learn what it is, where it's used, and whether it's safe.

E-numbers are codes for food additives approved in the European Union and UK. This database provides detailed information about each additive, including safety information based on scientific research.

Colours (E100-E199)

Natural and synthetic food colourings

E100
Curcumin

Natural yellow colour from turmeric. Used in butter, cheese, mustard.

Generally Safe
E102
Tartrazine

Synthetic yellow dye. Linked to hyperactivity in children.

Consider Avoiding
E110
Sunset Yellow

Orange-yellow synthetic dye. Similar concerns to tartrazine.

Use Caution
E150a
Plain Caramel

Brown colour from heated sugar. Found in cola and sauces.

Generally Safe
E150c
Ammonia Caramel

Caramel colour containing 4-MEI. Used in beer and soy sauce.

Use Caution
E150d
Sulphite Ammonia Caramel

The "cola caramel" - highest 4-MEI levels but reformulated.

Use Caution
E160a
Carotene

Natural orange colour, precursor to vitamin A.

Generally Safe
E171
Titanium Dioxide

White colour BANNED in EU. UK still allows. DNA damage concerns.

Avoid If Possible
E172
Iron Oxides

Natural mineral-based red/yellow/black colours.

Generally Safe
E133
Brilliant Blue

Synthetic blue dye in sweets. Requires hyperactivity warning.

Caution for Children
E131
Patent Blue V

Synthetic blue dye. Allergic reactions possible.

Use Caution
E132
Indigo Carmine

Synthetic blue dye. Generally well-tolerated.

Generally Safe
E141
Copper Chlorophyllin

Natural-derived green colour from plants.

Generally Safe
E153
Vegetable Carbon

Black charcoal colour. Can interfere with medications.

Caution with Meds

Preservatives (E200-E299)

Extend shelf life by preventing microbial growth

E211
Sodium Benzoate

Common preservative in soft drinks and pickles.

Use Caution
E220
Sulphur Dioxide

Preservative in dried fruit and wine. May trigger asthma.

Use Caution
E250
Sodium Nitrite

Preservative in processed meats. Linked to health concerns.

Consider Avoiding
E251
Sodium Nitrate

Curing salt in dry-cured meats. Converts to nitrite in body.

Use Caution

Antioxidants (E300-E399)

Prevent oxidation and rancidity

E300
Ascorbic Acid

Vitamin C. Used as antioxidant in many foods.

Safe (Vitamin)
E301
Sodium Ascorbate

Vitamin C sodium salt. Less acidic form in processed meats.

Safe (Vitamin)
E306
Tocopherols

Vitamin E. Natural antioxidant in oils and fats.

Safe (Vitamin)
E322
Lecithin

Emulsifier from soy or sunflower. Found in chocolate.

Generally Safe

Emulsifiers & Thickeners (E400-E499)

Stabilise texture and improve consistency

E420
Sorbitol

Sugar alcohol in sugar-free products. Laxative effect in large amounts.

Digestive Caution
E476
PGPR

Emulsifier in chocolate. Safe but indicates lower quality.

Generally Safe

Raising Agents (E500-E599)

Help baked goods rise

E503
Ammonium Bicarbonate

Traditional raising agent in biscuits. Completely safe.

Completely Safe

Flavour Enhancers (E600-E699)

Boost existing flavours in food

E621
MSG (Monosodium Glutamate)

Flavour enhancer in savoury foods. Safe but some sensitivity.

Use Caution

Sweeteners (E900-E999)

Low or zero-calorie sugar alternatives

E951
Aspartame

Artificial sweetener in diet drinks. WHO: "possibly carcinogenic".

Use Caution
E955
Sucralose

Artificial sweetener. Considered safe by regulators.

Generally Safe
E960
Stevia

Natural plant-based sweetener. Generally well-tolerated.

Generally Safe
