What is E440 (Pectin)?

E440 is pectin, a natural polysaccharide (complex carbohydrate) found in fruit cell walls, used as a gelling agent.

How it's made:

  • Extracted from apple pomace (leftover pulp from juice making)
  • Also from citrus peel (orange, lemon)
  • Boiled in acidic water, filtered, and dried

Why it's used:

  • Gelling agent ‚Äì makes jam set
  • Thickener ‚Äì creates viscosity in fruit preparations
  • Stabilizer ‚Äì prevents separation in acidic drinks
  • Soluble fibre ‚Äì adds dietary fibre

Where is E440 Found?

Jams & Preserves (Most Common):

  • Jam, marmalade, and jelly
  • Fruit spreads

Other Foods:

  • Yogurt (thickener)
  • Fruit drinks and smoothies
  • Desserts and puddings
  • Confectionery (fruit gums)
  • Bakery fillings

Naturally High in Pectin:

  • Apples (especially peel and core)
  • Citrus fruits (especially pith)
  • Quince, plums, gooseberries

Health Benefits of Pectin

Pectin is a soluble fibre with proven health benefits:

1. Lowers Cholesterol

  • Binds to cholesterol in the gut, reducing absorption
  • Studies show 5-10g daily pectin lowers LDL cholesterol by 7-10%
  • May reduce heart disease risk

2. Blood Sugar Control

  • Slows digestion and sugar absorption
  • Reduces post-meal blood glucose spikes
  • Helpful for diabetics

3. Gut Health

  • Prebiotic effect ‚Äì feeds beneficial gut bacteria
  • May reduce diarrhea (bulks stool)
  • Supports healthy gut microbiome

4. Weight Management

  • Increases satiety (feeling full longer)
  • Slows gastric emptying
  • May aid weight loss efforts

5. Detoxification

  • Binds to heavy metals in the gut
  • May help remove toxins from body
