Why Nutrition Tracking Matters

Calories tell you how much energy you're eating. But they don't tell you if you're getting enough Vitamin D, iron, or calcium. You can eat 2,000 calories of junk food and hit your calorie target while becoming seriously deficient in essential nutrients.

This is especially important in the UK, where many people are deficient in Vitamin D (due to limited sunlight), iron (especially women), and B12 (especially vegetarians and vegans).

Nutrition Tracker FAQs

What is the best nutrition tracker app UK?

NutraSafe is one of the best nutrition trackers for UK users because it tracks not just calories and macros, but all essential vitamins and minerals. Most calorie counters ignore micronutrients entirely. NutraSafe shows your daily intake of vitamins A, B, C, D, E, K, iron, calcium, zinc, and more.

Can I track vitamins with MyFitnessPal?

MyFitnessPal's free version only tracks basic macros (protein, carbs, fat). Detailed micronutrient tracking requires Premium at ¬£9.99/month. NutraSafe includes vitamin and mineral tracking in the free tier.

What nutrients should I track daily?

Beyond calories and macros, the most important nutrients to track are: Vitamin D (especially in the UK), iron, calcium, vitamin B12, zinc, magnesium, and fibre. NutraSafe automatically tracks all of these from the foods you log.

How do I know if I have a nutrient deficiency?

Common signs include fatigue (iron, B12), weak bones (calcium, vitamin D), slow healing (vitamin C, zinc), and muscle cramps (magnesium, potassium). NutraSafe helps you spot consistently low intake before symptoms appear. For diagnosis, consult your GP who can arrange blood tests.

Is nutrition tracking free with NutraSafe?

Yes, NutraSafe includes vitamin and mineral tracking in the free tier. You can log up to 5 foods per day and see complete nutrient information for each. Premium unlocks unlimited logging and historical trends.
