What Is Intermittent Fasting?

Intermittent fasting (IF) is an eating pattern that cycles between periods of fasting and eating. Rather than focusing on what you eat, intermittent fasting focuses on when you eat.

Popular in the UK for weight management, metabolic health, and digestive benefits, intermittent fasting has become one of the most widely practised eating patterns.

Benefits of Intermittent Fasting

Research suggests intermittent fasting may offer several health benefits:

  • Weight management: Helps create a calorie deficit naturally
  • Metabolic health: May improve insulin sensitivity
  • Digestive rest: Gives your digestive system time to rest and recover
  • Mental clarity: Many people report improved focus during fasting windows
  • Simplified eating: Fewer meals to plan and prepare

Popular Intermittent Fasting Schedules

NutraSafe supports all major intermittent fasting protocols:

16:8 Method

Fast: 16 hours
Eat: 8 hours

The most popular method. Fast overnight and through the morning, eating between 12pm-8pm or 1pm-9pm.

18:6 Method

Fast: 18 hours
Eat: 6 hours

For more experienced fasters. Eating window typically 2pm-8pm or 3pm-9pm.

Tips for Successful Intermittent Fasting

Start Gradually

If you're new to intermittent fasting, start with a 12-hour overnight fast and gradually extend your fasting window. Many people find 16:8 easiest to maintain long-term.

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water during your fasting window. Black coffee and unsweetened tea are also acceptable during fasting periods.

Focus on Nutrition Quality

Use NutraSafe's nutrition tracking to ensure you're getting adequate protein, healthy fats, and micronutrients during your eating window. Quality matters when you have fewer hours to eat.

Listen to Your Body

Intermittent fasting isn't suitable for everyone. If you experience persistent discomfort, dizziness, or other concerning symptoms, consult your GP.
