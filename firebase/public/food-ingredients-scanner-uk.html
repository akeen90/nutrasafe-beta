Why Can't I See Ingredients in My Calorie App?

If you use MyFitnessPal, Lose It, or similar calorie counting apps, you've probably noticed something missing: they don't show you what's actually in your food.

These apps only display nutrition numbers — calories, protein, carbs, fat. But they never tell you:

  • What preservatives and additives are in the food
  • Which E-numbers are present
  • Whether there are artificial sweeteners or colours
  • The full list of ingredients you're consuming

The problem: You might be tracking calories perfectly, but have no idea you're eating a dozen additives, emulsifiers, and artificial ingredients every meal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there an app that shows food ingredients?

Yes, NutraSafe shows the complete ingredients list for every product you scan. Unlike calorie counting apps like MyFitnessPal which only show nutrition numbers, NutraSafe displays every ingredient including additives, preservatives, E-numbers, and allergens.

Why doesn't MyFitnessPal show ingredients?

MyFitnessPal is designed for calorie and macro tracking, not ingredient transparency. Its database is crowd-sourced and users typically only enter calorie data. If you want to see what's actually in your food, you need an ingredients-focused app like NutraSafe.

Can I scan a barcode to see ingredients?

Yes, with NutraSafe you can scan any UK product barcode to instantly see the full ingredients list. It works with products from Tesco, Sainsbury's, Asda, Morrisons, Aldi, Lidl, and other UK supermarkets.

Is the food ingredients scanner free?

Yes, scanning products to see ingredients is completely free. You can scan unlimited barcodes and view full ingredients lists, additive safety ratings, and allergen warnings at no cost. Premium (£2.99/month) adds unlimited food diary logging.
