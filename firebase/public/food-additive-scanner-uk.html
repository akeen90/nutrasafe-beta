What Are Food Additives and E-Numbers?

Food additives are substances added to food to preserve flavour, enhance taste, or improve appearance. In the UK and EU, these additives are assigned E-numbers ‚Äî standardised codes that identify specific additives like preservatives, colourings, sweeteners, and emulsifiers.

Whilst many additives are safe, some have been linked to health concerns including allergic reactions, hyperactivity in children, and digestive issues. Understanding which E-numbers are in your food helps you make informed choices about what you eat.

Common Types of Food Additives

  • Preservatives (E200-E299): Prevent food spoilage and extend shelf life
  • Colourings (E100-E199): Add or restore colour to food products
  • Antioxidants (E300-E399): Prevent oxidation and rancidity
  • Sweeteners (E420-E499, E950-E969): Provide sweetness without sugar
  • Emulsifiers (E400-E499): Help mix ingredients that don't naturally combine
  • Flavour enhancers (E620-E650): Intensify taste, like MSG (E621)

Which Additives Should You Avoid?

Whilst regulatory bodies like the FSA (Food Standards Agency) approve additives for use, some may cause adverse reactions in sensitive individuals. NutraSafe helps you identify:

  • Artificial colourings: Some linked to hyperactivity in children (E102, E110, E122, E124, E129)
  • Preservatives: Can cause allergic reactions in some people (E220-E228, sodium benzoate)
  • Flavour enhancers: MSG (E621) and related compounds
  • Artificial sweeteners: Aspartame (E951), sucralose (E955), and others
  • Trans fats and hydrogenated oils: Linked to heart disease

NutraSafe doesn't just list these additives ‚Äî it explains what they are, why they're used, and what the research says about their safety.

Ultra-Processed Foods and Your Health

Ultra-processed foods contain multiple additives, preservatives, and industrial ingredients. Research links high consumption of ultra-processed foods to obesity, heart disease, and other health issues.

NutraSafe's additive scanner helps you identify ultra-processed foods by analysing:

  • Number and type of additives present
  • Processing level and industrial ingredients
  • Nutritional quality and density
  • Presence of artificial ingredients
