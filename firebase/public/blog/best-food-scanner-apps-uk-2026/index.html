Best Food Scanner Apps UK 2026: Which Should You Choose?

Published January 2026 • 12 min read • App Guides

Quick Answer

Best for UK users: NutraSafe (tracks vitamins + calories, UK food database, allergen warnings, £2.99/month). Best free option: Yuka (additive focus but French database). Largest database: MyFitnessPal (14M+ foods but many ads).

Food scanner apps let you scan barcodes and instantly see nutrition info, allergens, and additives — without squinting at tiny labels. But with dozens of apps available, which one actually works for UK supermarkets like Tesco, Sainsbury's, and Asda?

We tested 7 popular food scanner apps using 50+ products from UK supermarkets. This guide shows you which app is best for your needs, with honest pros/cons and feature comparisons.

7 Best Food Scanner Apps UK (2026 Rankings)

1. NutraSafe — Best for UK Users & Vitamin Tracking

Price: Free (5 scans/day) | £2.99/month unlimited

Best for: UK shoppers who want complete nutrition tracking beyond just calories

✓ Pros

How We Tested These Apps

We tested each app by scanning 50 products from UK supermarkets:

  • Tesco (20 products): Own-brand and branded items
  • Sainsbury's (15 products): Including Taste the Difference range
  • Asda (10 products): Budget and mid-range
  • Morrisons (5 products): Fresh and packaged

What we measured:

  1. Barcode recognition rate: % of products successfully scanned
  2. Data accuracy: Comparing app data to official nutrition labels
  3. UK database coverage: How many UK-specific products were found
  4. Feature completeness: Vitamins, allergens, additives, calories, macros
  5. Ease of use: Time to scan and log a product
  6. Value for money: Features vs price

Common Questions

Are food scanner apps accurate?

Apps pulling official product data (like NutraSafe and Nutracheck) are 95%+ accurate. Apps relying on user-submitted data (like MyFitnessPal) can have errors. Always check the source.

Can I track vitamins with a food scanner app?

Only NutraSafe tracks vitamins and minerals. MyFitnessPal, Yuka, and Nutracheck focus on calories/macros/additives only.

Which food scanner works best with UK supermarkets?

NutraSafe and Nutracheck have the best UK coverage. Yuka is French-focused and misses many UK products.

Is there a free food scanner app with no ads?

Yes: Yuka (completely free, no ads) and NutraSafe (5 scans/day free, no ads).

Can food scanner apps detect allergens?

NutraSafe highlights allergens with instant visual warnings. Set your allergies once and get automatic alerts when scanning products containing them.

Do I need to pay for a food scanner app?

No. Yuka is entirely free, and NutraSafe offers 5 free scans/day (£2.99/month for unlimited). MyFitnessPal is free but ad-heavy.
