20 High-Protein Low-Calorie Foods from UK Supermarkets (2026)

Published January 2026 • 12 min read • Nutrition

If you're trying to lose weight while preserving muscle, high-protein, low-calorie foods are your best friend. Protein keeps you full, supports muscle recovery, and has the highest thermic effect of all macronutrients (meaning you burn calories just digesting it).

This guide lists 20 of the best high-protein, low-calorie foods you can buy from UK supermarkets like Tesco, Sainsbury's, Asda, and Morrisons — with exact nutrition stats and meal ideas.

Why High-Protein Foods Are Essential for Weight Loss

Eating enough protein during a calorie deficit is crucial for several reasons:

  • Preserves muscle mass — Without adequate protein, you'll lose muscle along with fat, slowing your metabolism
  • Increases satiety — Protein keeps you fuller longer than carbs or fats, reducing cravings
  • High thermic effect — Your body burns 20-30% of protein calories during digestion (vs 5-10% for carbs/fats)
  • Supports recovery — If you're exercising, protein repairs muscle tissue

According to research, 1.6-2.2g of protein per kg of body weight is optimal for weight loss. For a 75kg person, that's 120-165g of protein per day.

What Counts as "High-Protein, Low-Calorie"?

For this list, we're using these criteria:

  • High-protein: At least 10g protein per 100g
  • Low-calorie: Less than 200 calories per 100g
  • Good protein-to-calorie ratio: At least 5g protein per 100 calories

How to Use These Foods for Weight Loss

Daily Protein Target

Aim for 1.6-2.2g protein per kg body weight when in a calorie deficit.

Example: 75kg person needs 120-165g protein/day

Sample Day (150g Protein, ~1,800 Calories)

  • Breakfast: 3 scrambled eggs (39g protein) + 1 slice wholemeal toast
  • Snack: Greek yogurt 0% (20g protein) with berries
  • Lunch: Tuna salad with 1 tin tuna (26g protein), mixed greens, olive oil
  • Snack: Protein shake (25g protein)
  • Dinner: 200g chicken breast (62g protein) with roasted vegetables and quinoa

Total: 172g protein, ~1,800 calories

Meal Prep Tips

  • Batch cook chicken breast on Sundays for the week
  • Hard-boil eggs for grab-and-go snacks
  • Pre-portion Greek yogurt into containers
  • Buy tinned tuna in bulk (shelf-stable convenience)
  • Freeze individual portions of cooked turkey mince

Final Tips for High-Protein Weight Loss

  • Spread protein across the day — 30-40g per meal optimizes muscle protein synthesis
  • Combine plant proteins — Mix lentils + rice or chickpeas + wholemeal bread for complete amino acid profiles
  • Don't fear fats — Salmon, eggs, and nuts contain healthy fats alongside protein
  • Track consistently — Use an app for 2-4 weeks to learn portion sizes, then you can estimate
  • Weigh food raw — Protein content changes when cooked (water loss)
