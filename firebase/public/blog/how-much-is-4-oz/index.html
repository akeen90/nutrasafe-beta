How Much Is 4 oz? UK Conversion Guide (Grams & ml)

Published January 2026 • 3 min read • Cooking

Quick Answer

4 oz = 113 grams (weight) or 120ml (liquid volume). The conversion depends on whether the recipe is measuring weight (oz for ounces) or fluid volume (fl oz for fluid ounces).

If you're following an American recipe or nutrition label, you'll often see measurements in "ounces" (oz). In the UK, we use grams for weight and millilitres for liquids, so you need to convert.

This guide explains exactly how much 4 oz is and provides a full conversion table for cooking and calorie tracking.

What Is an Ounce (oz)?

An ounce (oz) is an imperial unit of measurement used in the US and older UK recipes. There are two types:

1. Ounce (oz) — Weight

  • Used for solid foods (flour, sugar, chicken, cheese)
  • 1 oz = 28.35 grams
  • 16 oz = 1 pound (lb) = 454g

2. Fluid Ounce (fl oz) — Volume

  • Used for liquids (water, milk, juice)
  • 1 fl oz (US) = 29.57ml
  • 1 fl oz (UK imperial) = 28.41ml (rarely used today)

Important: Most modern recipes use US fluid ounces (29.57ml). When you see "4 oz" in a recipe, check the context:

  • "4 oz chicken breast" = weight (113g)
  • "4 fl oz water" = volume (120ml)

4 oz in Grams (Weight)

4 oz = 113.4 grams (round to 113g or 115g)

Common uses:

  • 4 oz chicken breast = 113g
  • 4 oz cheese = 113g
  • 4 oz butter = 113g
  • 4 oz flour = 113g
  • 4 oz sugar = 113g

4 oz in Millilitres (Liquid)

4 fl oz = 118ml (round to 120ml)

Common uses:

  • 4 fl oz water = 120ml
  • 4 fl oz milk = 120ml
  • 4 fl oz oil = 120ml
  • 4 fl oz juice = 120ml

How to Convert Ounces to Grams

Formula: Multiply ounces by 28.35

Examples:

  • 4 oz × 28.35 = 113.4g (round to 113g)
  • 6 oz × 28.35 = 170.1g (round to 170g)
  • 8 oz × 28.35 = 226.8g (round to 227g)

Quick mental math: 1 oz ≈ 30g (close enough for cooking)

How to Convert Fluid Ounces to Millilitres

Formula: Multiply fl oz by 29.57 (or use 30 for simplicity)

Examples:

  • 4 fl oz × 29.57 = 118ml (round to 120ml)
  • 8 fl oz × 29.57 = 237ml (round to 240ml)
  • 16 fl oz × 29.57 = 473ml (round to 475ml)

Quick mental math: 1 fl oz ≈ 30ml

Why the UK Uses Grams Instead of Ounces

The UK adopted the metric system in the 1960s-70s for several reasons:

  • More precise: 100g is always 100g. But "4 oz" can be 110-115g depending on rounding
  • Easier calculations: Metric uses multiples of 10 (100g, 250g, 500g)
  • International standard: Most of the world uses grams and litres
  • Better for nutrition: UK food labels show nutrition per 100g

Common Questions

Is 4 oz the same as 4 fl oz?

No. 4 oz = 113g (weight), while 4 fl oz = 120ml (liquid volume). They're different units measuring different things.

How much is 4 oz in cups?

4 fl oz = ½ cup (120ml). For dry ingredients measured by weight, it depends on density (see our guide: How Many Grams Is 1 Cup?)

What does 4 oz of chicken look like?

4 oz (113g) of chicken breast is roughly the size of a deck of cards or the palm of your hand (without fingers).

Is 4 oz a lot for a portion?

It depends:

  • Meat/fish: 4 oz (113g) is a moderate portion (3 oz = small, 6 oz = large)
  • Cheese: 4 oz (113g) is quite a lot (typical serving is 1-2 oz/30-60g)
  • Liquids: 4 fl oz (120ml) is a small glass (½ cup)
