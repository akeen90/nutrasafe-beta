How Many Grams in a Tablespoon? Complete UK Conversion Guide

Published January 2026 • 3 min read • Cooking

Quick Answer

1 tablespoon = 15ml for liquids, or 12-20g for dry ingredients depending on density. Water = 15g, sugar = 12g, flour = 9g, butter = 14g. A UK tablespoon is standardised at 15ml.

Recipes often call for "1 tablespoon of butter" or "2 tablespoons of flour," but how much is that in grams? The answer depends on what you're measuring, because tablespoons measure volume (space), not weight.

This guide shows you exactly how many grams are in a tablespoon for all common ingredients.

How Big Is a Tablespoon?

In the UK, a standard tablespoon (tbsp) is:

  • 15 millilitres (15ml) — the volume it holds
  • Equivalent to 3 teaspoons (5ml each)
  • Same volume as US tablespoons (both are 15ml)

Important: Use proper measuring spoons, not cutlery. A regular dinner spoon can be 10-25ml, which throws off recipes.

Why Grams Vary by Ingredient

Different ingredients have different densities (how heavy they are for the same volume):

  • Dense ingredients like honey or peanut butter weigh more per tablespoon
  • Light ingredients like flour or cocoa powder weigh less
  • Liquids have consistent weight-to-volume ratios

This is why 1 tablespoon of water = 15g, but 1 tablespoon of flour = only 9g.

Important: If using an Australian recipe in the UK, reduce tablespoon amounts by 25% (or use 3/4 of the stated amount).

How to Measure Tablespoons Accurately

For Dry Ingredients

  1. Spoon into the measuring spoon — don't scoop from the bag (compacts the ingredient)
  2. Level off with a knife — scrape excess off the top for an exact tablespoon
  3. Don't pack unless the recipe says "packed tablespoon"

For Liquids

  1. Pour liquid into the measuring spoon until it reaches the rim
  2. Check at eye level to ensure it's not overflowing
  3. For sticky liquids (honey, syrup), lightly oil the spoon first so it pours out cleanly

For Fats (Butter, Peanut Butter)

  1. Pack the fat into the spoon
  2. Level off the top with a knife
  3. For butter, 1 tablespoon = 14g (or use the markings on butter wrappers)

Why Grams Are Better Than Tablespoons

In the UK, professional bakers and nutritionists measure in grams because:

  • More accurate: 10g is always 10g. But "1 tablespoon of flour" can vary by 20-30% depending on how you scoop it
  • Consistent results: Recipes work the same every time
  • Easier for nutrition tracking: UK food labels show nutrition per 100g, making calorie counting simpler

Common Questions

Is a tablespoon the same as a dessert spoon?

No. A dessert spoon is 10ml (between a tablespoon and teaspoon). Always use proper measuring spoons for recipes.

How many tablespoons in a cup?

16 tablespoons = 1 cup (240ml). See our full guide: How Many Grams Is 1 Cup?

Can I use a regular soup spoon to measure?

No. Regular spoons vary from 10-25ml. Use a proper measuring spoon set for accuracy.

How do I measure half a tablespoon?

Half a tablespoon = 1½ teaspoons (7.5ml). Or use a proper ½ tablespoon measuring spoon.
