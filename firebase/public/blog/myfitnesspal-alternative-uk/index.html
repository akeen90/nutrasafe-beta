MyFitnessPal Alternative UK: 7 Better Calorie Counters (2026)

Updated January 2026 • 10 min read

MyFitnessPal has become bloated with ads, expensive subscriptions, and accuracy issues. We compare 7 better UK alternatives with accurate food databases, genuinely free features, and no intrusive advertising. Find your perfect calorie counter.

Why Look for a MyFitnessPal Alternative?

MyFitnessPal pioneered calorie counting apps and built an enormous food database. But in 2026, it's no longer the obvious choice for UK users:

  • Aggressive advertising: Constant interruptions with banner ads, video ads, and upgrade prompts
  • Expensive premium: £19.99/month or £79.99/year for basic features like meal planning
  • Poor UK database: Many UK supermarket products missing or using incorrect US data
  • Barcode scanner paywall: Essential scanning feature now locked behind subscription
  • Bloated interface: Packed with unnecessary features, social feeds, and sponsored content
  • Privacy concerns: Sold to Francisco Partners in 2020, extensive data collection practices

The good news? Several UK-focused alternatives offer better value, cleaner interfaces, and more accurate nutrition data for British foods.

Best MyFitnessPal Alternatives for UK Users (2026)

1. NutraSafe

Best for: Vitamin tracking, food safety, allergy warnings

9.2/10

NutraSafe goes beyond calorie counting to track ~25 vitamins and minerals, making it the only app that shows both macros and micronutrients side-by-side. Built specifically for UK supermarket products with instant barcode scanning and comprehensive allergen warnings.

Pros

  • Tracks vitamins & minerals (unique feature)
  • 5 free barcode scans per day (no subscription required)
  • Instant allergen warnings for your profile
  • Clean interface with no ads
  • Accurate UK database (Tesco, Sainsbury's, Asda)
  • Food additive analysis (E-numbers)
  • Apple Health integration

Cons

  • Newer app with smaller user database
  • iOS only (Android coming soon)
  • Limited social features
Pricing: Free with 5 scans/day. Premium £2.99/month for unlimited scans, full micronutrient tracking, and food reaction patterns.

2. Nutracheck

Best for: UK-specific calorie database, barcode scanning

8.8/10

Nutracheck is a UK-built calorie counter with an impressive database of British supermarket products, restaurant chains, and packaged foods. Their barcode scanner recognizes nearly every UK product without relying on US data.

Pros

  • Excellent UK food database (best accuracy)
  • Barcode scanner works brilliantly
  • Meal plans tailored for UK tastes
  • Clean, focused interface
  • Recipe importer for UK sites
  • Supportive UK-based community

Cons

  • No free tier (trial only)
  • Doesn't track micronutrients
  • Subscription required for all features
Pricing: 7-day free trial, then £3.99/month or £24/year. No genuinely free version.

3. Lose It!

Best for: Simple calorie tracking, habit building

8.5/10

Lose It! offers a cleaner alternative to MyFitnessPal with less aggressive advertising and a more streamlined interface. The free version is genuinely usable, though UK database coverage isn't as comprehensive as Nutracheck.

Pros

  • Clean interface with minimal ads
  • Barcode scanner (free tier)
  • Large food database
  • Habit tracking features
  • Photo food logging
  • Reasonable premium pricing

Cons

  • US-focused database
  • Many UK products missing
  • Premium required for macros
Pricing: Free with ads. Premium £39.99/year for macro tracking, meal planning, no ads.

4. Cronometer

Best for: Micronutrient tracking, precise nutrition data

8.7/10

Cronometer is the gold standard for nutrition tracking accuracy. It tracks 82 micronutrients using verified USDA and NCCDB data, making it perfect for serious nutrition optimization beyond simple calorie counting.

Pros

  • Tracks 82+ micronutrients (most comprehensive)
  • Extremely accurate nutritional data
  • Custom biometric tracking
  • No ads, clean interface
  • Export data to CSV
  • Keto, vegan, paleo diet support

Cons

  • Steeper learning curve
  • Smaller UK food database
  • Less social features
Pricing: Free (micronutrient tracking included). Premium £49.99/year for advanced features.

5. Yazio

Best for: Intermittent fasting, European users

8.3/10

German-built Yazio combines calorie counting with intermittent fasting tracking, making it ideal if you follow time-restricted eating patterns. Better European food database than US-focused apps.

Pros

  • Built-in fasting timer
  • Good European food database
  • Barcode scanner (free)
  • Recipe database with UK options
  • Water tracking
  • Clean, modern interface

Cons

  • Some UK products missing
  • Premium required for macros
  • Frequent upgrade prompts
Pricing: Free with limitations. Premium £34.99/year for full features.

6. Lifesum

Best for: Diet plans, recipe ideas, beautiful design

8.1/10

Lifesum focuses on structured diet plans (keto, Mediterranean, high protein) with recipe suggestions and meal planning. The app is beautifully designed but leans heavily towards premium features.

Pros

  • Gorgeous interface design
  • Structured diet plans
  • Recipe suggestions
  • Life score gamification
  • Macro tracking

Cons

  • Very limited free features
  • UK database gaps
  • Expensive premium
  • Barcode scanner locked behind paywall
Pricing: Free (very limited). Premium £44.99/year or £7.99/month.

7. FatSecret

Best for: Genuinely free features, budget-conscious users

7.9/10

FatSecret is one of the few remaining apps that offers genuinely useful features completely free. No paywall for barcode scanning, macro tracking, or meal planning. The catch? Ads and a dated interface.

Pros

  • 100% free (all features)
  • Barcode scanner included
  • Macro tracking free
  • Meal plans free
  • Large food database
  • Community recipes

Cons

  • Dated interface
  • Contains ads
  • UK database accuracy variable
  • Less polish than competitors
Pricing: Completely free with ads. Optional £7.99/month to remove ads.
