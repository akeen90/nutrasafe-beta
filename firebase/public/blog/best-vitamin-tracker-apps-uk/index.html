What to Look for in a Vitamin Tracker

  • Micronutrient coverage: Should track at least 20+ vitamins/minerals
  • Daily value percentages: Shows progress toward 100% RDA
  • UK database: Accurate nutrition for British products
  • Barcode scanning: Fast logging without manual entry
  • Gap identification: Highlights consistent deficiencies

Key Vitamins and Minerals to Track

Essential vitamins: Vitamin D, B12, B6, Folate, C, A, E, K

Essential minerals: Iron, Calcium, Magnesium, Zinc, Potassium, Iodine

Most people in the UK are deficient in Vitamin D (Oct-March) and Iron (women). These should be priority tracking targets.

Which App Should You Choose?

Choose NutraSafe if: You want comprehensive vitamin tracking (30+) with UK product barcode scanning and minimal complexity. Best for everyday UK users.

Choose Cronometer if: You want extreme detail (82 micronutrients) and don't mind a complex interface. Best for serious nutrition optimization.

Choose MyPlate if: You only care about tracking 5 basic vitamins (A, C, D, calcium, iron) and want it completely free.

Do You Really Need to Track Vitamins?

Track vitamins if:

  • You feel constantly tired despite good sleep
  • You follow restrictive diets (vegan, keto, carnivore)
  • You have diagnosed deficiencies (iron, B12, vitamin D)
  • You want to optimize athletic performance
  • You eat mostly processed foods

Skip vitamin tracking if:

  • You eat a varied, whole-food diet
  • You have no energy/health issues
  • You take a multivitamin covering common gaps
