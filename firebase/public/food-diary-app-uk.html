Why Keep a Food Diary?

A food diary is more than just a record of what you eat. It's a powerful tool for understanding your relationship with food, identifying patterns, and making lasting changes to your diet.

Research consistently shows that people who keep food diaries are more successful at losing weight and maintaining healthy eating habits. The simple act of writing down what you eat increases awareness and accountability.

But traditional food diaries are tedious. Who wants to count calories by hand or search through endless databases? NutraSafe solves this with barcode scanning, a comprehensive UK food database, and automatic nutrition calculations. Log a meal in seconds, not minutes.

Food Diary FAQs

What is the best food diary app UK?

NutraSafe is one of the best food diary apps for UK users. It features a comprehensive British food database, barcode scanning for UK products, and tracks not just calories but vitamins and minerals too. The app is free to download with no subscription required for basic features.

Is keeping a food diary good for weight loss?

Yes, research shows that people who keep a food diary lose more weight than those who don't. Writing down what you eat increases awareness of eating habits, helps identify problem areas, and keeps you accountable. NutraSafe makes food logging quick and easy with barcode scanning.

What should I track in a food diary?

A good food diary tracks what you eat, when you eat, portion sizes, and how foods make you feel. NutraSafe automatically calculates calories, protein, carbs, fat, fibre, and micronutrients. You can also log symptoms to identify food intolerances.

How long should I keep a food diary?

For best results, keep a food diary for at least 2-4 weeks to identify patterns. Many people find food logging becomes a lasting habit that helps them maintain healthy eating long-term. NutraSafe makes it easy enough to use every day.

Is NutraSafe food diary free?

Yes, NutraSafe offers a free food diary with barcode scanning and access to the UK food database. Free users can log up to 5 foods per day. Premium unlocks unlimited logging from ¬£2.99/month, but the free version is fully functional for basic food journaling.
